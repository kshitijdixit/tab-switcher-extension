Privacy Policy — Tab Switcher

Last updated: March 25, 2026

Overview

Tab Switcher is a Chrome extension that detects duplicate tabs and switches to existing ones instead of opening new copies. Your privacy is important to us, and this extension is designed to operate with minimal data access.

Data Collection

Tab Switcher does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data or browsing history. The extension does not use analytics, tracking, cookies, or any form of telemetry.

How the Extension Works

The extension reads the URLs of your open tabs in memory solely to determine whether a newly opened tab is a duplicate. This comparison happens entirely within your browser and no URL data is ever sent to any external server.

Local Storage

The extension saves your preferences (matching mode, per-site rules, and blocklist) using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.sync API. This data stays within your browser and Chrome account and is never accessed by the developer.

Third-Party Services

Tab Switcher does not use any third-party services, APIs, or external scripts.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, you can reach out by opening an issue on the GitHub repository.
